What is ADCS Active Defense Ôºä SOC Framework

ADCS is a framework for establishing a "pre-SOC foundation" based on the Active Defense approach.
It focuses on organizing three key areas before implementing expensive 24/7 SOC.

  • 1. Attack Surface Visibility from Attacker Perspective
    Using OSINT and Attack Surface Management to inventory organizational exposure from an attacker's viewpoint.
  • 2. Incident Response Preparation and Log Infrastructure
    Defining who does what during "the first 24 hours" of an incident, and organizing necessary logs and evidence collection methods.
  • 3. Step-up Design to Full SOC / MDR
    Organizing requirements and role divisions for smooth collaboration with full-scale SOC providers like IIJ or Ray-SOC when needed.

This provides a realistic and technical option between "doing nothing" and "multi-million yen full SOC."

Framework Components

Active Defense Ôºä SOC Framework consists of four components.
Not 24/7 monitoring like full SOC, but a technical blueprint of "what should be prepared first."

‚ë† Attack Surface & OSINT Visibility

Using OSINT tools like Shodan/Censys to organize how your organization appears to attackers.

  • Inventory of public servers, ports, and services

  • Email domain configuration (SPF/DKIM/DMARC) verification
  • Extract information forming the basis of attack scenarios

Taking the attacker's perspective to organize scenario-based "where and how you're vulnerable."

  • Create attack scenario assumptions (password, VPN, SaaS, etc.)
  • Prioritize misconfigurations and weaknesses
  • Improvement proposals from red team perspective

Playbook to avoid confusion during "the first 1-24 hours" of an incident, and define minimum log infrastructure.

  • Initial response step checklist
  • Reporting templates for internal, executive, customer, and related organizations
  • Types of logs to secure and retention policies
